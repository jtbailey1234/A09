Chainsaw Man: Reze Arc | 9.9/10

Even compared to non animated films Chainsaw Man: Reze Arc is seriously one of the best experiences I've ever had watching a movie. Reading the story through the manga and then watching the movie right after took this experience to the next level. Even though I knew what was going to happen the anticipation of seeing how it'd be adapted to real time fluid movement had me on the edge of my seat. The nuance of this story and how it handles broken people trying to learn how to love again is so beautiful. The soundtrack alone elevates this movie so much but all together this becomes a once in a life time experience. The only reason I didn't give this movie a 10/10 is because I believe nothing is perfect, but this was as close as you can get. I don't think this is a series for everyone but I still feel that most people will be able to resonate with the themes and characters.

Attack on Titan | 9/10

This show feels like it was written from the ending to the beginning. Every little detail ends up foreshadowing things that have yet to come even in the first 30 seconds of the first episode. While watching you're expected to come up with your own theories of the world as you and the characters are still uncovering all of the mysteries and it leads to some of the most insane reveals of any series ever. You'll go from thinking you understand most of what the series has to offer to having everything you thought you knew being a lie. With it's themes of imperialisim and war you learn about your own human nature and where you'd draw the line. This might sound like an easy question but do you think genocide is ever the correct answer? Well the end of Attack on Titan will have you battling with yourself over that question. The reason I only gave this series a 9/10 is because I felt some of the best parts of this series are hidden behind too many layers of metaphors and symbolisim to the point where it sort of loses it's original message.

My Hero Academia | 8/10

Even though the fandom for this series can be very terrible I believe if you put that aside this series truely is very great. It has some very deep stories involving unstable families, unhealthy comparisons, and what it means to be good or evil. There really isn't a single villian in this show that doesn't almost feel justified in their actions. Broken children who haven't felt love being neglected by society are of course not going to make the best decisions and are going to be preyed apon by bad people to do terrible things. The reason I gave this series an 8 is because I think it has a slow start, that is still enjoyable in it's own way, and a little bit of a fall off for season 5-6. Going into the final few seasons however, the series really does become a work of art. Throughout the show the heros have a catch phrase "go beyond, plus ultra!" and I kid you not during the final fights I found myself jumping out of my chair and yelling the phrase with them. This show does a phenomenal job at making you feel the hype of fighting for good and afterwards will make you wish superheros were real.
