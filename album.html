I Thought You Didn't Even Like Leaving - Prince Daddy & the Hyena

7.8/10

This album took a formative role in my personal development in high school. I would listen to most of this album every single day on the way to and from school. It is a staple in what emo music should sound like. Abraisive guitars and vocals that really sound like the singer is giving it his all as if he doesn't plan on singing ever again. The themes are very juvenile and come from a place of emotional immaturity but I think that's where it gets it's appeal. Listening to this makes me feel like a dumb teenager in high school again and that's why it holds a special place in my heart. I think overall though it is lacking in some production quality and has some songs that could've been fleshed out more.

Spring Break! - Harrison Gordon

8.5/10

While in my last review I mentioned how that album just sounds like my high school years well I feel like I can say the same with this album except for my college years so far. With themes about having bad roommates, being the bad roommate, the unfairness of life, but having a good time regardless it really feels like this artist understands my experiences. This album has such infectious hooks and lyrics that just won't leave your head. I think production and substance wise this album has no apparent flaws I just wish there was more of it. It only has 6 songs with one being a short instrumental intro to the album. Also a fun fact is I played D&D with the lead singer, Harrison Gordon, a few years ago right after they released their debut album "The Yuppies Are Winning" and I can confirm he's a super chill dude.

I Used To Go To This Bar - Joyce Manor

7/10

In this album Joyce Manor definietly leans into the pop side of their pop-punk sound. Overall I actually think this change is fun and a good direction for this band to go in their later years. They've already released about 5 albums that have that youthful edge and bite and I feel like if they tried to do that now it'd feel like a bunch of uncles trying to be cool. My issue though is that some of these songs feel lazy and overly repetitive. There's a running joke where Joyce Manor is scared of writing a song over 3 minutes because typically they're very fast and to the point but that doesn't mean they should just repeat the same verse for a minute and a half and call it quits. I can't lie a lot of the singles from this album are earworms but I just wish they spent more time on the other songs from the album.
